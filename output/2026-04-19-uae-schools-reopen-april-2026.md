## UAE Schools Set for In-Person Learning Resumption

Beginning Monday, April 20, 2026, all public and private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools), [nurseries](https://www.schoolsery.com/nurseries), and kindergartens across the UAE will resume in-person classes. This decision follows a nationwide directive from education authorities aimed at restoring traditional learning environments amid ongoing security concerns that previously necessitated a shift to distance learning.

### Phased Approach to Reopening

The reopening will not be uniform across all institutions, as schools in [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) must obtain approval from the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) before welcoming students back. This process may involve inspections to ensure compliance with safety protocols and operational readiness. As such, some schools may not be ready to reopen on the specified date, reflecting a phased approach tailored to each institution's specific circumstances.

### Adjustments in School Timings

On the first day of reopening, many schools will implement staggered start times, with arrivals scheduled between 7:00 AM and 10:30 AM. This adjustment is designed to facilitate a smooth transition back to on-campus learning, allowing schools to conduct final checks and orientation activities during the initial hours.

Parents are encouraged to stay informed about transportation arrangements, as bus services are expected to align with these new timings. However, parents should verify specific schedules with their respective transport providers, as routes may be subject to change.

### Safety Protocols and Emergency Procedures

To ensure the safety of students and staff, schools have established clear protocols. In the event of an emergency alert during school hours, students and staff will be directed to designated safe zones away from windows. Teachers will carry out rapid attendance checks, and trained supervisors will oversee these areas until an official all-clear is given.

It is important to note that parents will not be allowed to collect their children during an active alert. Instead, students will remain secured on campus until the situation is resolved, and families are advised against contacting schools during such incidents to allow staff to focus on safety measures.

### Focus on Reintegration and Emotional Support

The initial week of in-person learning will concentrate on structured reintegration. This will include age-appropriate safety awareness sessions and emergency drills intended to familiarise students with procedures without causing alarm. Schools aim to prioritise emotional reassurance and routine-building to help students adjust back to the classroom environment.

### Extracurricular Activities and Hybrid Learning

While outdoor activities will remain restricted initially, indoor physical education and some extracurricular activities may resume, provided they comply with the KHDA guidelines. Moreover, schools are permitted to adopt hybrid or rotational learning models under certain conditions, but this requires regulatory approval and demonstration of readiness.

### Preparing for Future Changes

The recent shift to online learning, which began on March 2 due to regional security concerns, has highlighted the necessity for schools to be adaptable. As circumstances evolve, schools must remain prepared to switch back to remote learning if required. This flexibility is crucial for managing potential disruptions in the academic year.

### What This Means for Parents

For parents navigating school choices in the UAE, the reopening of schools signifies a return to normalcy while also introducing new considerations regarding safety and operational flexibility. Parents should engage proactively with school administrations to understand how these changes impact their children’s educational experience. The emphasis on safety protocols and the phased reopening approach reflects a commitment to ensuring that students can learn in a secure environment.

As families adjust to these developments, staying informed about their chosen institution's policies will be essential in making informed decisions about their children's education in the coming months.
