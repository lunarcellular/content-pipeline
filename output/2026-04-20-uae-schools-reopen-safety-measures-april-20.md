## Schools in UAE Set to Reopen on April 20

As [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) across the UAE prepare to reopen on April 20, a renewed focus on safety and structured routines emerges in the wake of disruptions caused by the recent US-Israel-Iran war. Families are seeking assurance that schools will provide a safe environment for their children, prompting educational authorities and institutions to implement comprehensive safety measures.

## Emphasis on Safety and Wellbeing

The reopening is not merely about returning to classrooms; it signifies a commitment to rebuilding routines that prioritise student wellbeing. Educators are keen to establish clear communication channels and detailed emergency protocols to support students during this transition.

In [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai), institutions have closely aligned with the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) to ensure that readiness checks, staff training, and contingency plans are firmly in place ahead of the first school bell. Poonam Bhojani, CEO of Innoventures Education, highlighted that extensive operational and safety measures have been put in place to ensure a controlled return to in-person learning. This preparedness is driven by thorough risk assessments and strict regulatory compliance.

## Standardised Emergency Protocols

Schools in Dubai have standardised emergency procedures, including shelter-in-place, reverse evacuation, and evacuation protocols. These procedures are complemented by designated safe zones and assembly points to enhance student safety. Real-time accountability measures, such as register checks and controlled campus movement, will be in place to ensure that communication with parents remains clear while providing embedded wellbeing support throughout the school day.

Instilling confidence in parents is a priority. Lisa Johnson, principal of the American Academy for Girls, noted the importance of structured responses during emergencies. She explained that students and staff would swiftly move to designated safe zones, with teachers conducting rapid check-ins. Crucially, parents will not be allowed to collect their children during an emergency alert, as staff will be focused on ensuring student safety. Johnson urged parents not to call the school during active alerts to prevent disruptions.

## Adjustments to Daily Routines

To meet safety guidelines while maintaining a sense of normalcy, schools are modifying daily routines. Matthew Barrett, principal of Raffles International School, indicated that extensive planning has been undertaken to fulfil KHDA requirements. While assemblies and outdoor activities will be suspended, schools are working to ensure that students return to a familiar environment as much as possible. Safety procedures will be gradually introduced through brief orientations and planned drills.

Simon Jodrell, principal of Jebel Ali School, described a coordinated response system for emergencies. In such events, a 'shelter in place' procedure will be initiated, guiding students calmly to designated indoor safe areas while halting movement across the school. This coordinated approach ensures that registers are taken and that leadership, medical, and safeguarding teams oversee the process.

## Communication and Parental Guidance

Effective communication remains a central theme as schools maintain close contact with the relevant authorities until an "all clear" signal is issued. Structured systems during drop-off and pick-up will ensure that every student is safe and that families remain informed. Major educational operators, such as GEMS Education, are reinforcing a 'People First' approach, emphasising that student and staff safety takes precedence over all else. A circular from GEMS reminded families that in emergencies, lives are more important than belongings, advising against returning for personal items.

Students will be informed of the locations of safe zones within classrooms and common areas, which will be reachable within one minute. Schools are also encouraging families to rely solely on official alerts from school management and UAE authorities, discouraging reliance on social media or forwarded messages that may create panic.

## Conclusion: What This Means for Parents

The reopening of schools in the UAE on April 20 signals a significant step toward restoring normalcy in education. For parents, this means navigating a new landscape of safety protocols designed to protect their children while fostering an environment conducive to learning. Understanding the operational changes and emergency procedures in place will be crucial for families as they prepare for this next chapter in their educational journey. As schools embrace these new measures, parents can feel more assured about the safety and wellbeing of their children during their return to in-person classes.
