## UAE Schools Reopen with Enhanced Safety Measures

[Schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) across the UAE are set to reopen on Monday, April 20, after a period of disruption due to geopolitical tensions. This return comes with a series of new safety measures aimed at ensuring the wellbeing of students and staff. 

The reopening marks a significant step as families seek reassurance about the safety of classrooms amid ongoing global challenges. Schools are not only focused on reopening but are also dedicated to rebuilding a structured routine that prioritises student wellbeing. 

## Safety Protocols in Place

In [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai), schools have closely aligned with the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) to ensure readiness for the new academic term. Preparations have included extensive operational and safety measures. Poonam Bhojani, CEO of Innoventures Education, outlined that schools have conducted thorough risk assessments and staff training to facilitate a controlled return to in-person learning.

“Standardised procedures are in place across all campuses, including shelter-in-place, reverse evacuation, and evacuation protocols,” Bhojani said. Each school will implement designated safe zones and assembly points, with real-time student accountability through register checks and continuous supervision.

School leaders are emphasising clear communication to reassure families. Teachers will guide students to safe areas swiftly in the event of an emergency, with structured responses to manage each scenario effectively. 

## Adjusted School Routines

Principals across various schools have indicated that daily routines will be adjusted to meet safety guidelines while maintaining a semblance of normalcy. For instance, assemblies and outdoor activities are currently suspended to mitigate risks. Matthew Barrett, principal of Raffles International School, stated that students would be gradually introduced to safety procedures during the first days of school.

In light of these measures, families are encouraged to remain informed and adhere to school guidelines. Communication between the school and parents will be critical, especially during emergencies. Schools will be implementing controlled procedures during drop-off and pick-up to ensure every student is accounted for and safe.

## Parent Reassurance and Guidance

Several Dubai schools have advised parents to remain patient during emergency alerts, underscoring that during such times, they will not be able to collect their children. Effective communication is deemed vital during emergencies, and families are encouraged to rely on official updates from school management rather than social media channels, which may spread misinformation.

GEMS Education has reiterated a ‘People First’ approach, emphasising that the safety of students and staff is paramount. In a recent circular, the organisation reminded families that safety takes precedence over personal belongings during emergencies. 

Parents can expect ongoing collaboration between schools and regulatory bodies to enhance safety protocols further as the situation evolves. 

## Conclusion: Implications for Parents

The reopening of schools in the UAE, particularly in Dubai, signifies a commitment to not only resuming academic activities but also ensuring that safety remains a top priority. As schools implement enhanced safety protocols, parents are encouraged to stay engaged and informed about the measures being taken to protect their children. 

This proactive approach to safety may influence parental choices regarding schools, as families increasingly seek environments that prioritise student wellbeing in addition to academic excellence. The robust frameworks established by schools in response to the current climate will undoubtedly shape the educational landscape in the UAE going forward.
