## Schools Resuming In-Person Learning

[Schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) across the UAE are transitioning back to in-person learning, with students returning to classrooms as of April 20. This decision follows an extended period of distance learning prompted by regional uncertainties. The Ministry of Education's announcement marks a significant shift, aiming to restore a sense of normalcy in education.

The [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) Crown Prince, during a recent visit to the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA), reviewed the preparations for the return of students. This visit highlighted the authority's ongoing initiatives, which align with the Education 33 Strategy (E33). This strategy focuses on enhancing the quality of education in Dubai, promoting Arabic language and Emirati culture, and strengthening the collaboration between schools and parents.

## Key Initiatives and Support for Schools

During the Crown Prince's visit, he acknowledged the critical role played by the education community, including teachers and parents, in fostering high-quality learning environments. The emphasis on collaboration is pivotal in ensuring a smooth transition back to in-person classes. The KHDA has been actively working to implement standardised procedures to facilitate this shift, ensuring that schools are equipped to meet the needs of all students.

A notable feature of the educational landscape in Dubai is the formation of the Dubai Students Council, which serves as a voice for nearly 400,000 students across the emirate's private education sector. This council is comprised of 16 members selected from various private schools, reflecting a diverse representation of student perspectives. The council's establishment is a part of broader efforts to engage students in discussions about their educational experiences and to promote their interests within the schooling system.

## Implications for Parents and Students

The resumption of in-person learning is particularly significant for parents who have been navigating the complexities of remote education. The flexible return strategy allows families the option to choose between in-person and hybrid learning modalities, which is beneficial for those with students still abroad or with varying comfort levels regarding returning to school.

As schools prepare for this transition, parents are encouraged to stay informed about the guidelines and protocols being implemented. This includes understanding the measures in place to ensure the safety and well-being of all students during this period of adjustment. The proactive steps taken by the KHDA and the collaboration with educators indicate a commitment to maintaining educational standards while prioritising student safety.

Overall, the return to in-person learning in UAE schools is a considerable step towards normalising the educational experience for students and families alike. With authorities and schools working together to enhance educational quality and ensure a supportive environment, parents can feel confident about the upcoming academic term.
