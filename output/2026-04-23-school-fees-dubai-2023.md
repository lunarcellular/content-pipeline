## Understanding School Fees and Charges in Dubai This Term

As [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) resume in-person classes after a period of disruption, many parents are grappling with concerns regarding school fees and transport charges. With the backdrop of recent geopolitical tensions affecting classroom learning, the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) has provided important clarifications for families navigating this complex landscape.

### Tuition Fees During Distance Learning

Despite some schools continuing with distance learning, parents are required to pay the full tuition fees. KHDA has confirmed that schools delivering an approved distance learning programme are not mandated to reduce or waive any portion of these fees. The authority stated that fees remain payable for any period during which the educational service is available, whether it is provided in-person or through distance learning.

Parents should be aware that opting not to engage with remote learning does not justify withholding payment. Any dissatisfaction with the online learning experience must be addressed through the school’s official complaints process, and fees will still be due during this period.

### Conditions for Refunds

Refunds on school fees are available only in specific circumstances. KHDA stipulates that schools must compensate parents for any period in which no educational service is provided at all. This can include options such as credit notes, transfers to siblings, or full refunds. The timing of withdrawal requests is critical, as the date of submission will impact the refund calculations. Delays in submitting these requests could result in reduced refunds.

### Transport Charges and Adjustments

Transport fees are generally charged as an annual service rather than on a daily basis. However, with the changes in school operations, some transport providers are reassessing their fee structures. For instance, certain companies have announced reductions in transport fees, while others are adjusting charges based on actual usage during the transition back to in-person learning.

Previously, some educational institutions had waived transport fees when distance learning was mandated, demonstrating a willingness to support families during times of disruption. However, such measures are not guaranteed under the current policies, and parents should verify the terms with their respective transport providers.

### Financial Planning for the New Term

As families prepare for the new academic term, it is essential to budget for additional back-to-school expenses. Costs can vary significantly based on the child’s needs, with some parents spending a few hundred dirhams on uniforms and supplies, while others may incur expenses approaching Dh2,000 per child. Factors influencing these costs include the necessity of purchasing uniforms from designated suppliers and the potential for mark-ups in prices.

Additional expenses may also involve technology needs, such as laptops or tablets, especially as digital learning continues to play a role in education. Parents should anticipate these costs and incorporate them into their overall financial planning for the term.

### Conclusion

With the resumption of in-person education, understanding the implications of school fees and associated charges is paramount for parents in Dubai. The clarifications provided by KHDA aim to alleviate some of the uncertainties surrounding financial commitments during this transitional period. By staying informed and proactive in addressing financial obligations, families can better navigate the educational landscape amidst ongoing challenges.
