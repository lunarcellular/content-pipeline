## UAE Schools Confirm Holiday Plans for Eid Al Adha Break

As the UAE approaches the Eid Al Adha holidays in May, [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) have clarified their holiday plans, emphasising that the academic calendar will remain largely intact while allowing for some flexibility. This announcement comes amid growing inquiries from parents and students regarding potential adjustments to the academic schedule during this culturally significant period.

Education authorities and school leaders are working to balance the importance of cultural observance with the need to maintain academic continuity, especially following recent disruptions caused by international events. Schools are focused on helping students smoothly transition back to in-person learning, emphasising the importance of instructional time and student wellbeing.

## Flexibility in the Academic Calendar

While the academic calendar is mostly fixed, schools in the UAE have some discretion regarding holiday adjustments. For instance, schools can grant up to three additional days off at their discretion, provided they inform the relevant educational authorities. For example, if the official Eid holidays fall on a Monday to Wednesday, schools may choose to declare Thursday a holiday to extend the break to a full week, depending on their local weekend schedule.

However, leaders stress that any decisions must be made carefully to avoid disrupting academic plans. Maintaining learning continuity is essential, and schools may need to implement compensatory measures such as Saturday classes or longer school hours, pending approval from educational authorities.

## Prioritising Academic Preparedness

Schools with examination cohorts, particularly those following specific curricula, are prioritising academic preparation as the Eid holiday approaches. For some institutions, the focus is on ensuring that students are well-prepared for upcoming examinations, which are scheduled between mid-May and early June. Structured learning support and digital continuity measures are being reinforced to address any learning gaps that may have emerged due to recent disruptions.

Many schools are employing data-driven instructional strategies to ensure that all students are adequately prepared for their assessments. They are also utilising digital platforms for guided assignments and reinforcement strategies during the holiday period, where appropriate.

## Emphasising Wellbeing and Support

In addition to academic considerations, schools are placing a significant emphasis on the wellbeing and emotional readiness of students as they prepare to return to in-person learning. The transition back to classrooms is being meticulously managed to ensure that students feel safe, valued, and supported. This is particularly important after the challenges posed by recent events, which have underscored the need for robust support systems within educational environments.

Overall, schools across the UAE are committed to fostering an environment that prioritises both academic success and student wellbeing. As Eid Al Adha approaches, parents can expect schools to maintain a balance between celebrating this important cultural occasion and ensuring that their children's educational needs are met.

## Conclusion

The plans for the Eid Al Adha break highlight the adaptability of schools in the UAE as they navigate the complexities of cultural observance and academic demands. Parents should remain informed about their school's specific arrangements as the holiday approaches, ensuring that their children benefit from both a meaningful celebration and a focused academic environment.
