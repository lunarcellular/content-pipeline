## Dubai School Safe Zones: Understanding the New Requirements

[Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) have resumed in-person learning with new safety protocols aimed at ensuring student safety during emergencies. Following a period of remote learning caused by regional tensions, schools have embraced a phased return to normal operations. The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) has introduced specific requirements for schools to create designated ‘safe zones’ within their campuses.

### New Reopening Regulations

Schools in Dubai were permitted to reopen only after securing approval from KHDA. This included signing a reopening Acknowledgment and Consent Form and ensuring all staff completed training on the new protocols. Schools were given until April 27 to resume face-to-face learning alongside ongoing distance learning options.

### What Are Safe Zones?

Safe zones are crucial components of the reopening strategy. These designated areas are designed to protect students and staff during emergencies. To comply with KHDA guidelines, safe zones must meet several criteria:

- **Construction Specifications**: Safe zones must be enclosed by solid walls, have a concrete roof, and ensure no exposure to external areas.

- **Accessibility**: Each safe zone should be reachable within one minute from any location within the school, without the need to cross outdoor or exposed areas.

- **Ventilation and Safety**: Proper ventilation must be maintained, without any exposure to potential hazards. Emergency exits and assembly points should be clearly marked.

- **Multiple Locations**: Schools must provide multiple safe zones distributed throughout the building to facilitate quick access during an emergency.

- **Training and Preparedness**: Staff are required to be trained on safety protocols and evacuation routes, and emergency go-bags must be prepared and maintained, containing first aid supplies and contact details.

### Resumption of Outdoor Activities

As part of the reopening measures, many schools are beginning to resume outdoor activities, including sports and physical education classes. Initially, outdoor areas remained closed even after students returned to classrooms; however, recent guidance from KHDA indicates a gradual reopening of these facilities, allowing for a return to normal school routines.

### Implications for Parents

The introduction of safe zones and new safety protocols reflects a significant commitment to student welfare as schools navigate the complexities of reopening in a post-pandemic environment. Parents can take comfort in knowing that schools are implementing structured measures to protect their children during emergencies. This proactive approach may also influence parents' choice of schools as they seek institutions prioritising safety and compliance with regulatory guidelines.

In summary, the new safe zone requirements and the resumption of outdoor activities signal a positive step toward restoring confidence in the school environment. As these measures take effect, parents are encouraged to stay informed about their children's school policies and the ongoing efforts to ensure a secure learning atmosphere.
