## Upcoming Decision on Remote Learning in UAE

The Ministry of Education is set to announce an important decision regarding the future of remote learning in UAE [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools). This announcement will take place on Sunday evening, May 10, following an assessment of the current educational landscape in coordination with relevant authorities. 

Currently, schools and universities across the Emirates have been conducting online classes due to renewed safety concerns, including threats stemming from recent geopolitical tensions. This transition to remote learning was implemented as a precautionary measure to ensure the safety and well-being of the educational community.

The ministry has affirmed the readiness of educational institutions to switch between different learning models as needed. This flexibility is crucial in light of the evolving situation, and parents can expect updates through official communication channels.

### Context of Remote Learning Implementation

The shift to remote learning came after the government mandated this change at the beginning of March, coinciding with the onset of increased regional tensions. Since then, schools and [nurseries](https://www.schoolsery.com/nurseries) have been working to adapt to these challenges, with many reopening on April 20 under strict safety protocols. 

During the period of remote learning, important assessments and examinations, including international A-level and GCSE exams, were cancelled, impacting many students’ academic plans. Instead, educational institutions are focusing on alternative methods to evaluate student performance, such as coursework submissions and teacher-predicted grades.
