## In-Person Learning Resumes Across UAE

UAE [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) will resume in-person learning on May 11, following a brief period of remote education that was implemented as a precautionary measure due to heightened regional tensions. This decision comes from the Ministry of Education, which has coordinated closely with relevant authorities to ensure a smooth transition back to classrooms for students, teachers, and administrative staff in both public and private institutions.

The temporary shift to remote learning affected educational institutions from May 5 to May 8, which was necessitated by emergency alerts linked to missile and drone interceptions by UAE air defence systems. With the return to in-person classes, schools and [nurseries](https://www.schoolsery.com/nurseries) are expected to continue adhering to established safety and security protocols to safeguard the health and well-being of all participants in the educational process.

## Protocols and Preparedness

The Ministry of Education has confirmed that educational institutions must maintain strict safety measures and remain prepared to implement alternative learning models if required, based on ongoing assessments of the regional situation. This flexibility is crucial for ensuring that academic assessments and the overall educational process remain uninterrupted.

The Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research has also echoed this approach for universities and higher education institutions, which will similarly reopen for in-person learning. The coordination efforts between these ministries and educational institutions aim to facilitate a seamless return to campus life for students and staff across the UAE.

## Context and Implications for Parents

This resumption of in-person learning is significant for parents as it marks a return to a more traditional educational environment after months of adjustments and adaptations to remote learning. The earlier shift to online education had implications for student engagement and academic performance, which many parents were concerned about. With schools reopening, parents can expect a more structured learning experience for their children, which may help alleviate some of the challenges associated with remote learning.

However, parents should remain informed about the safety measures that schools will continue to enforce. The ongoing monitoring of the regional situation indicates that flexibility will be key in maintaining educational continuity. Further updates regarding any changes in protocols or educational delivery methods will be communicated through official channels, ensuring that parents have access to the most current information regarding their children's education.

As schools in the UAE prepare to welcome students back into classrooms, it is essential for parents to stay engaged with their children's educational journey and support the measures that schools are implementing to ensure a safe and effective learning environment.
