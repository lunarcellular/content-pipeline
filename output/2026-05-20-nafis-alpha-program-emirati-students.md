## Introduction of the Alpha Program

The Emirati Talent Competitiveness Council (ETCC), in collaboration with the Nafis Youth Council, has launched the second batch of the Nafis Alpha Program, aimed at equipping Emirati middle school students with vital skills for future job markets. This initiative reflects a commitment to nurturing a generation that is prepared for the evolving economic landscape.

## Objectives and Structure of the Program

The Alpha Program is designed to instil a work culture aligned with the private sector among Emirati students from an early age. It seeks to provide participants with insights into potential career paths, enhancing their confidence and skill set. The program consists of two distinct phases:

1. **Interactive Workshops**: The first phase, which commences immediately, includes workshops held at various educational institutions in [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) and Al Ain. These workshops will cover essential skills such as resume writing and interview preparation, facilitated by members of the Nafis Youth Council.

2. **Job Simulation**: Following the workshops, students will engage in a five-day job simulation. This phase allows students to gain first-hand experience in a real-world work environment, partnering with prominent private-sector companies. This exposure is intended to bolster students' confidence and practical skills.

## Collaborating with Industry Leaders

To deliver this programme effectively, ETCC has partnered with leading companies, including well-known firms in various sectors. This collaboration not only enhances the program's relevance but also ensures that students receive guidance and insights from industry experts.

## Alignment with National Vision

The Alpha Program is part of a broader strategic initiative aimed at preparing Emirati students for a competitive job market. It aligns with the UAE leadership's vision of economic transformation, ensuring that participants develop critical competencies that will make them attractive candidates in the labour market. The initiative is particularly significant given the ongoing changes in job demands and the evolving nature of work in the UAE.
