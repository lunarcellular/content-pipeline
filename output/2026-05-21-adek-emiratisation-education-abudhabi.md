## ADEK and Education Provider Form Strategic Partnership

The [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK) has announced a strategic partnership with a major education provider aimed at significantly increasing Emiratisation within the education sector. This collaboration is designed to implement a structured talent development roadmap, targeting a substantial enhancement in the representation of UAE Nationals in key educational roles.

This agreement marks a transformative approach to the recruitment and development of Emirati teaching talent, establishing a comprehensive framework that allows the education provider to lead in recruiting, deploying, and offering career progression opportunities for UAE Nationals. ADEK's role will focus on creating entry pathways for top talent and fostering the development of qualified Emirati candidates through targeted training initiatives.

## Focus on Teacher Development

The partnership builds upon ADEK's recent teacher talent strategy, which emphasises the importance of teachers in achieving educational excellence. The initiative seeks to strengthen teacher standards and professional development while empowering existing educators and attracting passionate individuals into Abu Dhabi's classrooms.

Central to this effort is the Abu Dhabi Educators’ Proficiency Framework, which defines the professional standards expected of teachers. This framework aims to enhance the quality of education by ensuring that educators possess the necessary skills and qualifications.

Through this partnership, the education provider will implement a structured recruitment pathway specifically for UAE Nationals who complete ADEK's Kon Moallim programme—a postgraduate diploma in education. Additionally, ADEK's Midad programme, a self-paced teaching bootcamp, will help aspiring educators build foundational skills, with opportunities for advanced candidates to progress into more comprehensive teaching qualifications.

## Advancing National Priorities

This collaboration also aligns with broader national objectives to increase the presence of Emiratis in teaching roles, particularly in subjects like Social Studies, which the UAE government aims to have occupied exclusively by Emirati teachers within three years. The partnership will play a crucial role in developing a sustainable pipeline of high-calibre teaching talent across various disciplines, including Special Educational Needs (SEN) and early childhood education.

The education provider has committed to a multi-year talent development programme that aims to introduce new Emirati educators into the workforce by the 2030/2031 academic year. This initiative is seen as vital for ensuring that the UAE's educational landscape reflects its national identity and meets the needs of a rapidly evolving society.

## Implications for Parents and Students

For parents considering educational options in Abu Dhabi, this partnership signifies a robust commitment to quality education and the importance of Emirati representation within classrooms. The focus on recruiting and developing local talent is likely to enhance the educational experience for students, providing them with relatable role models and culturally relevant perspectives.

As the education sector continues to evolve, parents can expect to see an increasing emphasis on local talent, which could foster a more inclusive and supportive learning environment. This strategic partnership not only addresses the immediate needs of the education sector but also lays the groundwork for a sustainable future where Emiratis play a central role in shaping the educational landscape.

In conclusion, ADEK's collaboration with a major education provider signals a significant step towards enhancing Emiratisation in education, ensuring that the UAE's future educators are well-prepared to meet the challenges of tomorrow while reinforcing the national vision for sustainable development.
