## Introduction of the IQRA Programme

A new Arabic literacy programme, known as IQRA, is set to be implemented in private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in Ras Al Khaimah from September 2026. This initiative aims to strengthen foundational reading skills among young learners, specifically targeting children in KG1 through Grade 1.

## Evaluation Results

An independent evaluation has revealed that the IQRA programme significantly enhances early Arabic literacy. The findings indicate that participants in the programme made reading gains equivalent to an additional 25% of a school year. This evaluation was conducted through a large-scale randomised controlled trial by J-PAL MENA during the 2024–2025 academic year, encompassing 83 classrooms across 26 schools.

## Addressing Literacy Challenges

The IQRA programme addresses persistent challenges in Arabic education, particularly the gap between everyday spoken Arabic and Modern Standard Arabic. Many children face difficulties due to the complexities of Arabic, including new vocabulary and unfamiliar sentence structures. The programme simplifies reading instruction through a structured, step-by-step approach, enabling young learners to build confidence and fluency.

Unlike many educational interventions, IQRA does not rely on additional school hours or costly technology. It is designed to be integrated into the existing school timetable, requiring minimal teacher training.

## Programme Development and Methodology

Developed with insights from cognitive psychology, the IQRA programme focuses on key areas of reading development, including phonics, decoding, and reading fluency. The methodology is structured to gradually build children's reading skills. Instructional materials include large-font workbooks and guided reading techniques, fostering an environment where students can practice reading in a supportive manner.

## Implementation and Impact

The ease of implementation of the IQRA programme has been highlighted as one of its key strengths. It was delivered within regular classroom hours in the majority of participating schools, thus avoiding additional strain on school schedules. Research has shown that this programme leads to measurable improvements in areas such as letter identification, word reading, and reading fluency.

Overall literacy performance improved significantly, with students demonstrating robust gains in various literacy measures. The median student in the IQRA classrooms moved from the 50th to the 54th percentile in overall literacy performance.

## Future Prospects

The IQRA programme has also been tested in various other countries, including Morocco, Egypt, and Jordan, suggesting its potential for scalability and adaptability across different educational contexts. The successful implementation in Ras Al Khaimah could serve as a model for other regions seeking to enhance Arabic literacy among young learners.

## Conclusion

The rollout of the IQRA programme in Ras Al Khaimah marks a significant step forward in addressing early literacy challenges in Arabic. By focusing on evidence-based teaching methods and integrating literacy instruction into existing school frameworks, this initiative has the potential to foster a generation of confident and proficient Arabic readers. This development will be particularly relevant for parents considering educational options that prioritise strong foundational language skills in their children.
