## Abu Dhabi's Commitment to Emiratisation in Education Sector

The [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK) has formalised a strategic partnership with a major private education provider aimed at significantly increasing the representation of UAE Nationals in teaching roles across the emirate. This initiative is part of a broader effort to enhance Emiratisation in the education sector, with the goal of expanding the involvement of Emiratis in key educational positions within private-operated [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools).

### A Landmark Agreement

This collaboration marks a historic first between ADEK and a private education entity, implementing a structured talent development roadmap that anticipates a ninefold increase in Emirati representation in teaching roles. This strategic partnership underscores the UAE's commitment to fostering a strong national teaching workforce, aligning with the educational priorities outlined by the government.

ADEK will support this initiative by creating entry pathways for talented UAE Nationals and enhancing the preparation and training of qualified Emirati candidates. This approach reflects a recognition of teachers as pivotal figures in driving student success and achieving national educational objectives.

### Building a Sustainable Talent Pipeline

Central to the partnership is the establishment of a comprehensive framework for recruitment, career progression, and development opportunities for Emirati teachers. The programme includes the Kon Moallim initiative, which offers a postgraduate diploma in education designed to equip UAE Nationals with the necessary skills and confidence to excel in teaching roles. Additionally, the Midad teaching bootcamp will serve as an entry-level programme, allowing individuals to explore the field of education and develop foundational teaching skills.

This structured approach aims to build a sustainable pipeline of high-calibre teaching talent by ensuring that successful candidates from ADEK's programmes have clear pathways into teaching careers within private schools in Abu Dhabi.

### Addressing National Priorities

The partnership is strategically aligned with national priorities, particularly the aim to fill social studies teaching roles with Emiratis over the next three years. This focus extends beyond social studies to other critical areas, including special educational needs and early childhood education, recognising the diverse competencies required in different educational disciplines.

The overarching goal is to strengthen the presence of UAE Nationals in all educational settings, thereby enhancing the quality of education and fostering a deeper connection to the national identity within classrooms.

### Implications for Parents and Communities

For parents considering schooling options for their children in Abu Dhabi, this initiative signifies a growing commitment to educational excellence and the development of a qualified Emirati teaching workforce. The strategic focus on Emiratisation may also influence the diversity and cultural relevance of the curriculum, ensuring that students are educated in an environment that reflects the values and heritage of the UAE.

As schools in Abu Dhabi adopt these new frameworks, parents can expect to see an increased emphasis on educational quality, as well as enhanced support for Emirati teachers, which could contribute positively to the overall learning experience for their children.

In summary, this partnership between ADEK and a major education provider not only aims to transform the teaching landscape in Abu Dhabi but also reinforces the UAE's commitment to developing its human capital through education. As this initiative progresses, it will likely play a pivotal role in shaping the future of education in the emirate, making it an important consideration for families navigating the educational landscape.
