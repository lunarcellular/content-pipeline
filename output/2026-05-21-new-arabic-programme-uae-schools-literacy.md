## New Arabic Programme in UAE Schools Promises Enhanced Literacy

A new Arabic literacy programme is set to be introduced across private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in Ras Al Khaimah starting September 2026. Named IQRA, which translates to "read" in Arabic, this initiative aims to significantly boost reading skills among young learners.

The IQRA programme has been developed by a research foundation focused on education policy and has undergone rigorous independent evaluation. The results indicate that children participating in the IQRA programme achieved reading progress equivalent to an additional 25% of a school year compared to their peers following the standard Arabic curriculum. This evaluation involved 83 classrooms across 26 schools during the 2024–2025 academic year, marking it as one of the largest studies of early Arabic literacy in the UAE.

## Measurable Gains in Literacy

Students enrolled in IQRA classrooms demonstrated notable improvements in various literacy skills, including letter identification, word reading, non-word decoding, and oral reading fluency. Specifically, the median student’s performance in word reading improved from the 50th to the 58th percentile, while overall literacy gains raised the median student from the 50th to the 54th percentile. These advancements were observed across both native and non-native Arabic speakers, showcasing the programme's inclusivity.

One of the core challenges in Arabic education is the phenomenon of diglossia—the difference between the colloquial Arabic spoken at home and the Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) used in educational settings. IQRA addresses this issue by restructuring the reading instruction process into clear, manageable steps that prioritise phonics, letter recognition, decoding, and fluency before advancing to more complex reading tasks.

## Effective Teaching Strategies

The programme is designed to be seamlessly integrated into existing curricula without requiring additional resources or extended instructional time. In 21 of the 26 schools involved in the trial, IQRA was implemented within the regular school timetable, with only a few days of training necessary for teachers to effectively utilise the programme.

Lessons in IQRA employ structured workbooks featuring large fonts and carefully sequenced instruction, alongside teaching techniques such as paired reading, echo reading, and choral reading. These methods are specifically crafted to enhance reading fluency and build student confidence progressively.

## Addressing the Needs of Young Learners

The programme is tailored for children from Kindergarten to Grade 1 and aligns with the UAE Ministry of Education's Arabic curriculum. Its goal is to strengthen and complement existing Arabic instruction rather than replace it.

The evaluation of IQRA revealed that improving teaching methods can yield significant learning benefits without necessarily increasing resources. This shift in pedagogy is crucial, as how children are taught can dramatically influence their educational outcomes.

Furthermore, the programme has been successfully trialled in other countries, including Morocco, Egypt, and Jordan, showing promise for helping older students struggling with reading difficulties. This positions IQRA as a potential model for enhancing Arabic literacy across the region.

## Implications for Parents

The introduction of the IQRA programme in schools across Ras Al Khaimah represents a significant step towards improving literacy outcomes for young Arabic learners. For parents, this initiative not only provides hope for better reading skills in their children but also aligns with broader educational objectives set forth by local authorities.

As the programme rolls out, parents can expect their children to benefit from a structured and effective approach to learning Arabic, ultimately enhancing their overall educational experience. This initiative reaffirms the commitment of educational authorities to improve literacy rates and address the unique challenges posed by the teaching of Arabic language.
