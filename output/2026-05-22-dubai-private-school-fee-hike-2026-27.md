## Dubai Private School Fees Remain Unchanged for 2026-27

[Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai)'s Knowledge and Human Development Authority has confirmed that private school fees will not increase for the 2026-27 academic year, a decision aligned with a wider effort to support families amid ongoing economic challenges. This directive follows the guidance of the Dubai Crown Prince, who emphasised the importance of stability within the private education sector.

As part of a broader economic incentive package valued at Dh1.5 billion, which aims to bolster various sectors in Dubai, this move reflects the government's commitment to maintaining affordability in education. The total value of support measures for the education sector has now reached Dh2.5 billion, encompassing 33 initiatives designed to enhance the stability and competitiveness of private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools).

### Support Measures for Private Schools

These measures include several key benefits for KHDA-regulated private schools, such as deferred or instalment-based licence renewal fees and exemptions from various fees and fines imposed by Dubai Municipality. Additionally, the Knowledge Fund Establishment will provide partial rent exemptions and extended rent-free periods for centres under development, helping to ease financial pressures on educational institutions.

Moreover, schools will benefit from partial or full exemptions from guarantee insurance requirements and the suspension of contractual penalty clauses. These steps are crucial for ensuring continuity and stability across Dubai's private education sector while balancing the interests of both families and institutions.

### Current Educational Landscape in Dubai

Currently, more than 95% of students are engaged in in-person learning at private schools, demonstrating the sector's resilience and operational readiness despite recent challenges, including disruptions caused by geopolitical tensions. In the current academic year, approximately 9,000 new affordable school places have been introduced, contributing to a total enrolment of around 230,000 students in affordable education options.

Plans are underway to add an additional 7,500 school places over the next two years, further enhancing accessibility to quality education. The KHDA is also reviewing over 30 applications for new private schools, highlighting ongoing growth in the sector.

The Dubai private education landscape offers 17 different curricula options, catering to diverse learning needs and preferences. This variety ensures that families have ample choice when it comes to selecting an educational path for their children.

### Implications for Families

For parents in Dubai, the decision to maintain current tuition costs provides a measure of financial relief in an environment where many families are facing economic uncertainty. The stability of school fees, combined with the introduction of new affordable school places, underscores the government's commitment to education as a priority area, ensuring that families can access quality education without the burden of rising costs.

As the education sector continues to evolve, these measures are designed to support the long-term sustainability and growth of private schooling in Dubai, reinforcing its status as a leading global destination for quality education. Parents can feel more secure in their education choices, knowing that the government is actively working to support both families and educational institutions during these challenging times. 

The proactive stance taken by the Dubai authorities not only aids in stabilising the education market but also enhances the attractiveness of the emirate as a hub for families seeking quality educational opportunities for their children.
