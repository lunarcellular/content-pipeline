## Introduction of the Iqra Programme

An innovative Arabic learning programme, named Iqra, is set to be expanded across all private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in Ras Al Khaimah in the upcoming academic year. This initiative aims to improve the literacy skills of young learners by focusing on phonetics and reading fluency rather than traditional grammar-based instruction. The expansion follows a successful trial conducted in 83 classrooms across 26 schools, where the programme demonstrated significant improvements in Arabic literacy.

## Objectives and Methodology

The Iqra programme, developed by a prominent educational foundation, prioritises foundational reading skills such as fluency and decoding. Unlike conventional approaches that often emphasise comprehension and memorisation, Iqra employs methods such as repeated oral reading, paired reading, and choral reading. These techniques are designed to enhance students' comfort with written Arabic and to foster a more effective learning environment.

The initiative addresses a critical issue known as Arabic diglossia, where children’s home language differs significantly from Modern Standard Arabic, the form taught in schools. Many students struggle with reading fluency due to this discrepancy, which can lead to delays in literacy development. The programme aims to bridge this gap by providing structured exposure to written Arabic from the earliest stages of education.

## Supporting Data and Expected Outcomes

The Ministry of Education has mandated that private schools in the UAE provide Arabic language instruction for kindergarten-age pupils for a minimum of 40 minutes daily. This requirement is part of a broader strategy to promote the Arabic language among young learners. By the 2027-2028 academic year, the instruction time is expected to increase to 300 minutes per week.

An independent evaluation of the Iqra programme revealed that students participating in it made Arabic reading progress approximately 25% faster than their peers following the standard curriculum. The results showed statistically significant gains in various literacy metrics, including letter identification and reading fluency, particularly in word reading, which is a foundational skill for overall literacy.

## Implications for Parents and Schools

For parents considering educational options for their children, the expansion of the Iqra programme presents a promising development. It signifies a commitment to enhancing Arabic literacy within the educational framework in Ras Al Khaimah. The focus on effective teaching methods that resonate with how children learn to read could lead to better educational outcomes in the long term.

As schools prepare to implement this programme, parents can expect to see improved literacy skills in their children, aligning with the national push to strengthen Arabic language education.
