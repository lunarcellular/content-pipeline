## Dubai Private School Fees Frozen

[Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) families can breathe a sigh of relief as the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) has announced a freeze on private school tuition fees for the 2026-27 academic year. This decision aims to alleviate financial pressures faced by families amid rising costs of living.

The KHDA's announcement reflects its ongoing commitment to supporting families and ensuring stability within the education sector. This fee freeze means that no increases will be applied to tuition costs in Dubai [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools), providing a much-needed reprieve for parents.

### Fee Structure and Inspection Ratings

Typically, private schools in Dubai adjust their fees based on the Education Cost Index (ECI) and their most recent inspection ratings. Schools that maintain high inspection ratings are allowed to increase fees in line with the ECI, while those with lower ratings face restrictions on fee increases. However, this year’s freeze means that for the upcoming academic session, schools will not be permitted to raise tuition fees, regardless of their inspection outcomes.

During the past two academic years, Dubai schools did not undergo full inspections by the Dubai Schools Inspection Bureau (DSIB), with only certain newer schools completing their third year of operation being inspected. KHDA has focused instead on targeted quality assurance visits aimed at school improvement priorities. It remains uncertain whether full inspections will resume in the next academic year, which could affect the fee adjustments in subsequent years.

### Wider Economic Support Package

The fee freeze is part of a broader economic incentives package valued at approximately Dh1.5 billion. This initiative includes measures designed to ease operational pressures on families, schools, and early childhood centres. Among the support measures are the deferral of licence renewal fees, postponement of fines for KHDA-licensed institutions, waivers on selected licence renewal charges for [nurseries](https://www.schoolsery.com/nurseries), and freezes on planned rent increases.

These initiatives demonstrate a concerted effort to maintain stability within the education sector and offer financial relief to families navigating increased economic pressures.

### Additional Costs to Consider

While the freeze on tuition fees offers immediate financial relief, parents are encouraged to review other costs associated with re-enrolment. Schools may still revise additional charges, such as registration and re-registration fees, transport, uniforms, extracurricular activities, and devices. These costs can vary significantly from school to school, and parents should be aware of these potential expenses as they prepare for the new academic year.

To further assist families, many schools are adopting flexible payment structures. This includes partnerships with fintech platforms to enable families to manage tuition payments through instalment options. Such arrangements can help to ease the burden of upfront costs, making education more financially accessible for parents.

### Conclusion

The KHDA’s decision to freeze private school fees for the 2026-27 academic year is a significant step in addressing the financial challenges faced by families in Dubai. As schools continue to adapt to the changing economic landscape, parents should remain vigilant about understanding all associated costs and payment options. This proactive approach will be crucial for families as they plan for the upcoming school year while navigating the complexities of the educational landscape in Dubai.
