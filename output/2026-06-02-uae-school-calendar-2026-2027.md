## Overview of the 2026–2027 Academic Calendar

The Ministry of Education has officially confirmed the school calendar for the 2026–2027 academic year, providing clarity for parents and [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) throughout the UAE. Classes will commence on **August 31, 2026**, and the academic year is set to conclude on **July 2, 2027**. This structured timeline is designed to balance instructional time with necessary breaks, offering a roadmap that simplifies planning for families.

## Key Dates and Breaks

### Start and End of Year

The academic year will begin with students returning to classrooms on **August 31, 2026**, after a long summer break. The year culminates with classes ending on **July 2, 2027**, marking nearly ten months of structured learning.

### Holiday Breaks

The academic calendar includes several scheduled breaks:
- **Mid-Term Break**: From **October 12 to October 19, 2026**, students will have a week off shortly after the school year begins. This early break allows students to recharge and prepare for the academic workload ahead.
- **Winter Break**: Starting on **December 14, 2026**, schools will close for the festive period, with classes resuming on **January 4, 2027**. This break offers families time to celebrate the holiday season together.
- **Spring Break**: Scheduled for **April 5 to April 9, 2027**, this break occurs just before the final term, allowing students a brief pause before the intensity of exams and assessments.

### Flexibility for Private Schools

While the announced calendar applies primarily to public schools and private institutions following the national curriculum, private schools with alternative curricula may have some flexibility in structuring their mid-term breaks. However, any adjustments must adhere to the overarching guidelines set forth by the Ministry of Education, ensuring that total break durations do not exceed five school days.

## Implications for Families

The early release of the academic calendar aims to facilitate long-term planning for families. By providing clear term dates and holiday periods, the Ministry of Education seeks to enhance operational readiness within schools and assist parents in organising travel, childcare, and academic activities effectively. This structured approach allows families to better coordinate their schedules around school commitments.
