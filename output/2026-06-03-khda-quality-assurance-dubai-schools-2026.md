## Resumption of Quality Assurance Visits

[Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai)'s Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) has announced a significant change in its approach to ensuring the quality of education in private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools). Starting in the 2026-27 academic year, KHDA will implement short-notice inspections, providing schools with no more than 24 hours' notice before a quality assurance team arrives.

This new initiative introduces two distinct types of visits: comprehensive inspections and shorter monitoring visits. The full inspections will be conducted by specialist teams and will result in a detailed report, including an overall rating for the school. In contrast, the monitoring visits will focus on specific areas of concern and provide concise feedback without issuing a new overall rating.

## Background and Objectives

This move to restart inspections follows a pause in the 2025-26 academic year, which was positioned as an opportunity for schools to engage in reflection and collaboration regarding educational quality. KHDA has stated that the resumption of these visits aims to support continuous improvement in schools, enhance student outcomes, and bolster parents' confidence in the education system.

The quality assurance activities are aligned with several strategic initiatives, including the Education 33 (E33) Strategy, which prioritises learner-centric approaches and aims to ensure high-quality education for all students in Dubai. This strategy is also part of broader goals set within the Dubai Plan 2033, which seeks to establish the emirate as a leading global education hub.

## The Inspection Process

Under the new framework, every eligible private school in Dubai will undergo one of the two types of visits. The comprehensive inspection will apply the full UAE School Inspection Framework and will include a thorough evaluation of various aspects of the school's operations. Schools that have been operational for three years will automatically receive this full inspection as part of their regular assessment.

The shorter monitoring visits will be conducted by smaller teams who will focus on specific audit trails and lines of inquiry identified through data analysis. Following these visits, schools will receive a report that highlights their strengths and offers recommendations for improvement.

KHDA's approach aims to create a realistic picture of school life, ensuring that inspections reflect the daily realities of teaching and learning. This method is designed to provide meaningful feedback to schools, helping them to make informed decisions based on genuine evidence rather than on the pressure of preparing for inspections.

## Impact on Schools and Parents

The return of quality assurance visits is expected to have a positive impact on both schools and parents in Dubai. By providing more frequent and authentic feedback, schools can better identify areas for improvement and implement effective strategies for growth. For parents, this renewed focus on quality assurance reinforces confidence in the educational choices available for their children.

KHDA has committed to collaborating closely with schools throughout this process, ensuring that the feedback received is constructive and aimed at fostering educational excellence. The authority will also evaluate the impact of schools' improvement efforts over the past two years during these visits, focusing on how they can continue to grow in light of recent developments in the education landscape.

In conclusion, the KHDA's resumption of quality assurance visits marks a pivotal step in reinforcing Dubai's commitment to high-quality education. As schools prepare for these inspections, parents can look forward to enhanced educational standards and improved outcomes for their children.
