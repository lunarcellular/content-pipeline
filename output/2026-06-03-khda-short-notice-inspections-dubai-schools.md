## KHDA to Implement Short-Notice Inspections for Dubai Schools

The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) in [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) is set to introduce short-notice inspections for private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) starting from the 2026-27 academic year. This new framework will require schools to prepare for visits from quality assurance teams with no more than 24 hours' notice, marking a significant shift in how educational quality will be monitored in the emirate.

### New Approach to Quality Assurance

Under this innovative approach, KHDA will conduct two types of quality assurance visits: full inspections and shorter monitoring visits. The full inspection will be carried out by a team of specialists using the UAE School Inspection Framework, resulting in a comprehensive report that includes an overall school rating. Conversely, the shorter monitoring visits will focus on specific areas of concern identified through data analysis, providing a concise report with strengths and recommendations but without a new overall rating.

The inspections aim to support continuous improvement in schools, enhance student welfare, and build parents' confidence in the education system. This initiative aligns with the broader goals of Dubai's Education 33 (E33) Strategy, which emphasises high-quality education for all students, particularly Emirati learners. It also contributes to the ambitions outlined in the Dubai Plan 2033 and the Dubai Economic Agenda (D33).

### Rationale Behind the Change

The resumption of inspections follows a pause during the 2025-26 academic year, which was described as an opportunity for schools and KHDA to engage in greater collaboration and reflection on educational quality. By adopting this short-notice inspection model, KHDA aims to provide a more authentic picture of school operations and teaching practices, reflecting the everyday realities of educational environments.

This method of inspection helps ensure that feedback is timely and relevant, enabling schools to respond to recommendations based on actual conditions rather than pre-prepared presentations. The approach is informed by best practices from leading global quality assurance bodies, thereby enhancing the credibility and effectiveness of the inspection process.

### Support for Continuous Improvement

In the lead-up to these inspections, KHDA has been actively collaborating with schools to reinforce quality standards. Over the past two years, the authority has provided schools with increased flexibility in their improvement processes. This has included reviewing Self-Evaluation Forms (SEFs) and analysing data from various assessments to monitor educational quality effectively.

Additionally, KHDA has implemented a school improvement programme designed to support institutions with significant numbers of Emirati learners, further underlining its commitment to enhancing educational outcomes across the board.

### Implications for Parents

For parents considering education options in Dubai, this renewed focus on inspections will be a crucial factor in evaluating schools. The short-notice inspections are intended to create a more transparent and reliable education environment, ultimately benefiting students' learning experiences. With these changes, parents can expect improved accountability and a stronger assurance of quality as schools work towards meeting the enhanced standards set forth by KHDA.

The emphasis on continuous improvement and authentic assessment will likely lead to better educational outcomes, which is a key consideration for families navigating the diverse school landscape in Dubai. As the city aims to position itself among the top ten global cities for education quality, these measures reflect a significant commitment to elevating educational standards across the emirate.
