## KHDA to Implement 24-Hour Notice Inspections for Dubai Schools

The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) has announced significant changes to the inspection process for private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai), starting from the 2026-27 academic year. Under the new framework, schools will receive inspections with no more than 24 hours' notice. This initiative aims to enhance educational quality and adapt to the evolving landscape of school performance assessment.

### Two Types of Quality Assurance Visits

KHDA's new inspection model introduces two types of quality assurance visits. The first type is a comprehensive quality assurance inspection conducted by a team of specialists. This inspection will utilise the full UAE School Inspection Framework, resulting in a detailed report, including an overall school rating.

The second type is a shorter monitoring visit, which will be carried out by a smaller team focusing on specific aspects of the school’s performance. This visit will produce a concise report highlighting strengths and recommendations for improvement; however, it will not result in a new overall inspection rating.

This change in approach follows a pause in inspections during the 2025-26 academic year, which KHDA described as a period for "greater collaboration and reflection" on educational quality. The authority emphasises that the resumption of inspections is designed to foster continuous improvement in schools, enhance student wellbeing, and bolster parental confidence in the education system.

### Alignment with Educational Strategies

The revised inspection framework is closely aligned with Dubai's Education 33 (E33) Strategy, which prioritises learner-centric education and aims to ensure high-quality educational experiences for all students, especially Emirati learners. Additionally, it supports broader ambitions outlined in the Dubai Plan 2033 and the Dubai Economic Agenda (D33), which aspire to position Dubai among the world's top ten cities for education quality.

### Authentic Assessment of School Performance

KHDA asserts that the new inspection approach will provide a more authentic representation of school operations and educational quality. By conducting visits with minimal advance notice, KHDA aims to reflect the everyday reality of teaching and learning rather than an environment heavily prepared for inspections. This strategy is expected to yield more meaningful feedback and recommendations for schools, ensuring that they are based on genuine evidence rather than pre-prepared presentations.

Over the past two years, KHDA has actively collaborated with schools to reinforce educational quality, providing flexibility for improvement processes and monitoring through various mechanisms. This includes ongoing engagement with school leaders, analysis of assessment results, and targeted support for schools with high numbers of Emirati learners.

### Looking Ahead

As the 2026-27 academic year approaches, the focus of quality assurance activities will be to evaluate the impact of schools' improvement efforts over the past two years. The authority aims to provide targeted support to ensure schools continue to evolve and enhance their educational offerings, particularly in light of recent regional developments.

Parents can view these inspection changes positively, as they are designed to strengthen confidence in the quality of education available in Dubai. The new framework not only addresses the diverse needs of schools at various stages of their improvement journey but also aligns with international best practices, reinforcing the commitment to delivering a world-class education for all students in the emirate.
