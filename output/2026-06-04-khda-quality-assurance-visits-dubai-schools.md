## KHDA to Resume Quality Assurance Visits for Dubai Schools

The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) is set to reinstate school quality assurance visits starting from the 2026–27 academic year. This initiative marks a renewed commitment to enhancing oversight across private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai), aiming to support educational improvement and bolster parental confidence in the education system.

The forthcoming assessment framework will diverge from the previous singular inspection model. Schools will now be categorized for either a comprehensive inspection or a more concise monitoring visit, tailored to their specific needs. This flexible approach is designed to provide a more accurate representation of day-to-day teaching and learning, moving away from a one-size-fits-all methodology.

## Key Features of the New Inspection Model

A significant aspect of the updated inspection regime is the introduction of short-notice visits, typically giving schools no more than 24 hours' warning before inspectors arrive. This strategy is intended to deliver a genuine snapshot of the school's daily operations, ensuring that evaluations reflect the actual learning environment rather than a rehearsed presentation.

The KHDA's decision to resume these visits comes after a two-year hiatus during which inspections were suspended. The only exceptions during this period were for newly established schools in their third operational year, which were assessed to ensure compliance with educational standards and to identify areas requiring support.

## Context and Importance of Quality Assurance

During the suspension of inspections, the KHDA continued to monitor schools through various channels, including quality assurance visits for new institutions and ongoing engagement with school leaders. This interim monitoring focused on how schools adapted to distance learning and maintained student and staff wellbeing amidst the challenges posed by the pandemic.

The resumption of quality assurance visits aligns with Dubai's broader educational vision encapsulated in the Education 33 (E33) Strategy. This strategy aims to place learners at the heart of long-term reforms, enhancing educational quality for all students while particularly focusing on Emirati learners. Furthermore, it supports the goals set out in the Dubai Plan 2033 and the Dubai Economic Agenda (D33), which aspire to position Dubai among the top ten cities globally for educational quality.

## Implications for Parents

For parents navigating the educational landscape in Dubai, the KHDA's renewed focus on quality assurance visits represents a positive step towards ensuring that schools meet high standards of education. By fostering continuous improvement and enhancing oversight, these visits are designed to empower parents with greater confidence in the quality of education their children receive.

Moreover, the emphasis on short-notice inspections is likely to encourage schools to maintain consistent quality in their teaching and learning processes throughout the academic year, rather than only preparing for scheduled inspections. This approach could lead to more authentic educational experiences for students, benefiting their overall development and wellbeing.

In conclusion, the KHDA's initiative to reintroduce quality assurance visits signifies a proactive approach to elevating educational standards in Dubai. As schools prepare for this new phase, parents can anticipate a more rigorous and transparent assessment process that supports ongoing improvement and aligns with their expectations for quality education.
