## Resumption of School Inspections in Dubai

The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) has announced that inspections at private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) will resume for the 2026-27 academic year. This decision aligns with the emirate’s commitment to enhance educational quality and is part of the broader Education 33 Strategy, which aims to place students at the centre of the education system.

Inspections were halted after the KHDA's last comprehensive report in 2024, which evaluated 209 schools. This report identified 23 schools as “outstanding” and 48 as “very good”, while also categorising others into “good”, “acceptable”, and “weak.” Since the suspension of full inspections, only schools in their third year of operation have been subjected to comprehensive evaluations.

## Types of Inspections

Starting in 2026, the KHDA will implement two distinct types of inspections for eligible schools. The first is a full quality assurance visit that employs the complete UAE School Inspection Framework. This assessment will culminate in a detailed report that includes an overall rating for the school.

The second type is a shorter monitoring visit, which will be conducted by a smaller team of experts. This visit focuses on specific areas of inquiry based on data analysis and will provide a concise report detailing strengths and areas for improvement, but will not issue a new overall rating.

To ensure authenticity, schools will receive no more than 24 hours’ notice before either type of visit. This approach is designed to reflect the real conditions of teaching, learning, and student well-being, aligning with international best practices adopted by leading educational quality assurance bodies.

## Implications for Parents

The resumption of inspections is significant for parents considering educational options for their children in Dubai. It enhances confidence in the educational quality of schools, as regular evaluations will provide insights into school performance and accountability. Parents will have access to updated ratings and evaluations, which can assist in making informed decisions regarding their children's education.

The KHDA's initiative also underscores the authority's commitment to continuous improvement in education standards across the emirate. With inspections returning, there is an expectation that schools will focus on addressing the areas identified for improvement in previous evaluations, thereby striving for better outcomes for students.

In conclusion, the reintroduction of inspections by the KHDA marks a pivotal moment for private education in Dubai. As the authority works in partnership with schools and the community, parents can look forward to more robust mechanisms for ensuring high-quality education for their children.
