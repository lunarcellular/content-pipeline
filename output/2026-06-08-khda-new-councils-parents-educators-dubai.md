## KHDA Expands Decision-Making Framework in Dubai Education

The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) is set to enhance community involvement in the [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai) education sector by introducing two new councils: the Dubai Parents Council and the Dubai Educators Council. This initiative, launching at the beginning of the 2026-27 academic year, aims to incorporate the perspectives of parents and educators into the decision-making processes within the private education system.

The establishment of these councils follows the successful implementation of the Dubai Students Council, which has played a pivotal role in engaging students in educational policy discussions. The new councils will consist of 15 members each, selected through a transparent application process. The KHDA reported notable interest within the community, receiving 152 applications for the Parents Council and 160 for the Educators Council, reflecting a diverse array of perspectives, including contributions from Emirati parents and educators.

## Objectives and Structure of the New Councils

The Dubai Parents Council and Dubai Educators Council are designed to serve as structured platforms for dialogue between [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) and the KHDA. Their primary role will be to support policy development that aligns with the Dubai Education 33 Strategy, which seeks to position the emirate as a global leader in education.

The KHDA emphasised that these councils aim to empower those closest to learners—parents and educators—to actively participate in shaping educational policies and practices. This initiative is rooted in the belief that effective collaboration among all stakeholders is essential for enhancing student outcomes.

## Importance for Parents and Educators

The introduction of these councils signifies a substantial shift in how educational policy is formulated in Dubai. Parents and educators will now have formal avenues to voice their opinions and contribute to the educational landscape, fostering a more collaborative environment. This move could lead to more tailored educational experiences that meet the needs of students and families.

Moreover, by involving parents and educators directly, the KHDA is working towards creating a more connected education system where community feedback plays a crucial role in shaping policies and initiatives. As these councils commence, it is expected that the insights gathered will drive positive changes across the educational spectrum.

## Conclusion

The establishment of the Dubai Parents Council and Dubai Educators Council marks a significant step in enriching the educational framework within Dubai. By empowering parents and educators to engage actively in the decision-making process, the KHDA is not only valuing their contributions but also enhancing the overall educational experience for students. This initiative, aligned with the broader Education 33 Strategy, aims to cultivate a more inclusive, responsive, and future-focused education system that prioritises the needs and perspectives of all stakeholders involved.
