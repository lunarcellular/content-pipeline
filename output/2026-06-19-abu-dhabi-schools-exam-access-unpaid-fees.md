## Key Policy Change on Exam Access for Abu Dhabi Students

In an important move to uphold students' rights to education, [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) are prohibited from denying students entry to examinations due to unpaid or overdue tuition fees. This directive from the Abu Dhabi Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK) aims to ensure that all students have equal opportunities to participate in their assessments, regardless of their financial circumstances.

## Structure for Fee Recovery

ADEK has clarified the procedures that schools must follow regarding outstanding tuition fees. Before taking any actions against families, schools are mandated to send three consecutive written reminders to parents, allowing at least one week between each notice. This structured approach is designed to provide ample opportunity for families to address any outstanding payments.

After the third reminder, schools have the authority to suspend a student’s registration for a maximum of three days within a term; however, this can only occur once per academic term. Importantly, despite these measures, schools are explicitly prohibited from barring students from taking their examinations due to unpaid fees.

## Financial Consequences for Schools

While students cannot be denied entry to exams, schools retain certain rights concerning the management of fees. They may withhold examination certificates, transfer certificates, or block student transfers via the Student Information System (eSIS) until all outstanding fees are settled. Additionally, schools can refuse to re-enrol a student if fees remain unpaid, provided that parents are given written notice at least three months before the academic year ends regarding the consequences of non-payment.

This policy, which has been effective since the start of the 2025-2026 academic year, reflects ADEK's commitment to maintaining a transparent and fair fee-collection process. Schools are required to publish a clear fee-collection policy on their websites, which should outline the procedures for managing late or non-payment.

## Future Directions in Abu Dhabi Education

ADEK has also outlined its strategic priorities for the upcoming phases of private education in Abu Dhabi. These strategies aim to enhance the education system in alignment with the UAE leadership's vision, focusing on the development of future-ready generations. Key areas of focus include reinforcing national identity and Emirati values, promoting student health and safety, fostering future skills, and ensuring high-quality, inclusive education centred on teacher excellence.

This multifaceted approach seeks to build a robust education system that is responsive to the challenges of the modern world while ensuring that students are equipped with the necessary skills to thrive.

## Implications for Parents

For parents navigating the complexities of school fees in Abu Dhabi, this new policy is a significant development. It ensures that financial barriers will not prevent their children from accessing critical examinations. However, it also underscores the importance of being proactive in managing tuition payments. Parents should remain informed about their school's fee policies and the potential impacts of unpaid fees to avoid any complications regarding their children's education.

As families consider the financial implications of private schooling, understanding these regulations can help in making informed decisions about their children’s educational journey in Abu Dhabi.
