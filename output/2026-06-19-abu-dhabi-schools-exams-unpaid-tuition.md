## Clarification on Exam Policies Amid Unpaid Tuition Fees

[Schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) are now prohibited from denying students the opportunity to take exams due to unpaid tuition fees, according to recent announcements from the emirate’s education regulator. This directive reinforces the students’ right to access education, a critical aspect of Abu Dhabi's educational framework.

The Abu Dhabi Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK) has established a structured process that schools must adhere to when addressing fee-related issues with families. Schools are required to send three consecutive written reminders to parents about any outstanding tuition fees, ensuring at least one week between each notice. This process aims to provide families with ample opportunity to address any financial obligations before more serious actions are considered.

After these reminders, schools can suspend a student's registration for up to three days within a term, but this action can only occur once per academic term. Importantly, ADEK has made it clear that students cannot be denied entry to examinations based on unpaid tuition fees. This measure is designed to uphold educational access, allowing all students to participate in assessments regardless of their financial situation.

### Policy Enforcement and Compliance

In addition to the above measures, schools may withhold examination certificates and transfer certificates until all outstanding fees have been settled. They can also block student transfers through the Student Information System (eSIS) and refuse re-enrollment if fees remain unpaid, provided that parents are notified in writing at least three months before the academic year concludes about the potential consequences of non-payment.

ADEK has mandated that all private schools in Abu Dhabi comply with this policy starting from the 2025-2026 academic year. This timeline allows schools to adjust their operations and ensures that they clearly communicate their fee-collection policies on their websites. These policies must be transparent and fair, detailing the processes in place for managing late or non-payment of tuition fees.

### Future Directions for Abu Dhabi Education

This initiative is part of a broader strategy by ADEK to enhance the quality of private education in the emirate. The authority has outlined four key priorities aimed at developing a more future-ready education system. These include strengthening national identity and Emirati values, enhancing student health and well-being, developing future skills, and fostering an inclusive education system centred on teacher excellence.

The shift in policy regarding unpaid tuition fees is significant for parents navigating the educational landscape in Abu Dhabi. With the assurance that their children will not be barred from examinations due to financial issues, parents can better plan their educational expenses and engage with schools on a more equitable footing. This approach not only reflects a commitment to education but also aligns with the UAE leadership’s vision to cultivate generations equipped for future challenges.

In summary, the new regulations enacted by ADEK are a step forward in ensuring that educational access remains a priority in Abu Dhabi, allowing all students the opportunity to succeed academically without the fear of financial barriers hindering their progress.
