## Overview of New Regulations for Abu Dhabi Schools

Private [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) are now mandated to permit students to sit for examinations even if there are outstanding tuition fees. This significant policy change has been implemented by the Abu Dhabi Department of Education and Knowledge (ADEK), which aims to protect students' educational rights.

## Key Policy Details

Under the new regulations, schools must adhere to a structured process when addressing issues related to fee arrears. Specifically, they are required to issue three consecutive warning notices to parents or guardians, with a minimum of one week between each notice. Only after these notices have been issued may a school consider suspending a student's attendance, and even then, this action can only occur once per academic term.

The regulations further stipulate that while schools can withhold examination certificates and transfer certificates, as well as block student transfers through the electronic Student Information System (eSIS), they must notify parents in writing at least three months before the academic year concludes about any potential consequences linked to non-re-enrolment due to unpaid fees.

These measures are set to be fully enforced starting from the 2025-26 academic year. Schools must also ensure that their fee collection policies are transparent, clear, and readily available on their websites, outlining the steps they will take in cases of late or non-payment.

## Implications for Parents and Students

This policy shift is particularly noteworthy for parents navigating the financial landscape of private education in Abu Dhabi. By ensuring that students cannot be excluded from examinations due to financial disputes, the AEDK aims to foster an inclusive educational environment. This move not only protects students’ right to education but also supports families who may face temporary financial difficulties.

Parents should be aware that while schools are restricted from denying exam access, they still have the right to take certain actions related to fee payment. This includes measures like suspending attendance and withholding important documentation, which could affect a student's ability to transfer schools or re-enrol.

## Context of the Changes

ADEK's announcement reflects a broader commitment to reforming the private education sector in Abu Dhabi. The department's strategy focuses on fostering Emirati identity and values, promoting student wellbeing, and equipping learners with the skills necessary to thrive in a rapidly evolving world. The approach is designed to address the educational needs of students while ensuring a high-quality and inclusive education system centred on excellence.

This initiative represents an important step in aligning educational practices with the overarching goals of the Emirati educational framework, particularly in light of the challenges posed by economic fluctuations and the need for resilience in the face of such changes.

## Conclusion

For parents of students in Abu Dhabi, understanding these regulations is vital as they navigate the complexities of private education. The assurance that their children will not be barred from examinations due to unpaid fees may provide some relief. However, awareness of the potential consequences of non-payment remains essential. As these policies come into full effect, schools will need to communicate clearly with families about their obligations and the support available to them, ensuring that the educational journey of students remains uninterrupted.
