## Introduction

As the academic year approaches its conclusion, [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) in the UAE are preparing for year-end examinations, which will take place from June 22 to July 3. To uphold the integrity of these assessments, the Ministry of Education has introduced new anti-cheating measures aimed at preventing examination misconduct across all schools following the Ministry's curriculum.

## Overview of Year-End Examinations

The Ministry of Education has established a comprehensive schedule for the year-end examinations for students in Grades 5 to 12. These exams will be conducted electronically, allowing students to attend their respective schools during this period. The electronic testing system is a significant step towards modernising assessments and ensuring a robust examination process.

## Anti-Cheating Measures

To combat cheating and ensure fairness, the Ministry has outlined several procedures for reporting and addressing examination violations. Five distinct channels have been created for students, educational staff, and external individuals to report misconduct.

### Reporting Channels

1. **Staff Violations**: Any misconduct by staff involved in administering examinations should be reported to school administration, which will initiate an investigation and potentially escalate the matter to the Human Capital Department.

2. **Confidential Reporting**: A confidential mechanism has been established for reporting cheating incidents. This includes an online link, an email address for the Student Assessment and Performance Evaluation Department, and a dedicated toll-free number.

3. **In-Hall Cheating**: Incidents of cheating detected within examination halls must be immediately addressed by the chief invigilator, who will document the incident and collect evidence for further investigation.

4. **External Individuals**: Violations by individuals not part of the Ministry's workforce will be investigated by school administrations and may be referred to legal authorities if necessary.

5. **Private Examination Personnel**: Any misconduct from examination personnel in private schools must also be reported to relevant departments to maintain consistent examination standards.

These measures reflect a rigorous oversight framework aimed at enhancing accountability and ensuring that all students have equal opportunities during examinations.

## Transition for Younger Students

For students in Grades 3 and 4, the traditional end-of-term examinations will be replaced by School-Based Summative Assessments (SSA) for the 2025-2026 academic year. This shift aims to provide a more holistic assessment approach for younger learners.

## Conclusion

The implementation of these anti-cheating measures is a proactive step towards enhancing the integrity of educational assessments in the UAE. For parents, this development signifies a commitment to maintaining high standards within the education system, ensuring that their children’s achievements are based on genuine merit. As schools adapt to these new regulations, families can feel more secure in the fairness of the assessment process and the educational environment within which their children are learning.
