## Expansion of UK Independent Schools in the UAE

The UAE's private education sector is set for significant growth with the announcement that one of the UK's leading independent [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools) will open branches in [Abu Dhabi](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Abu%20Dhabi) and [Dubai](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools?location=Dubai). Epsom College, established in 1855, plans to launch a K-12 campus in Abu Dhabi in September 2028, followed by a branch in Dubai two years later.

These new schools, catering to mixed-gender cohorts, are part of a broader partnership with a major education operator in the UAE, aimed at establishing a network of premium British curriculum schools across the region. Each campus is projected to accommodate between 2,000 and 2,500 pupils at full capacity and will offer a comprehensive educational experience from early years through to sixth form.

## Facilities and Educational Offerings

Epsom College Abu Dhabi is to be constructed on a purpose-built site designed to replicate the school’s historic grounds in Surrey. Plans for the campus include a sports centre featuring a 50-metre swimming pool, gyms, and multipurpose halls. Additionally, there will be facilities for sports such as football, cricket, tennis, basketball, and netball, alongside dedicated spaces for dining, music, and drama.

This expansion reflects a significant investment in the educational landscape of the UAE, aligning with the strategic vision to enhance the quality and accessibility of education in the region. The partnership aims to create a seamless pathway for lifelong learning, reinforcing the UAE's status as an emerging global education hub.

## Broader Educational Landscape

Epsom College is not the only UK institution making strides in the UAE. Other prestigious schools, including Harrow School and King’s College School Wimbledon, are also scheduled to open branches in Abu Dhabi and Dubai in the coming years. This trend indicates a growing demand for high-quality British education within the UAE, catering to both local and expatriate families.

King’s College School is set to open on Fahid Island in September 2028, with a capacity of 2,200 pupils, while Rugby School plans to welcome students in Dubai for the 2026-27 academic year, further expanding the British educational offerings in the region.
