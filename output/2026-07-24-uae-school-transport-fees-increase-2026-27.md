## Overview of Upcoming Transport Fee Increases

As families in the UAE prepare for the 2026-27 academic year, some will face an increase in school transport fees, which are set to rise by up to 5%. This change has been attributed to escalating fuel and service costs, prompting several operators to adjust their pricing structures.

## Details of the Fee Adjustments

Certain transport companies have communicated to families that the adjustments in fees are necessary to maintain the quality and reliability of their services. These increases are not uniform across all providers; rather, they are determined on an individual basis depending on specific operational needs and contractual agreements with [schools](https://www.schoolsery.com/schools).

For instance, one transport provider has indicated that parents using their services must re-register their children by a specific deadline to secure their seats for the upcoming school year. This indicates an effort to streamline operations while also addressing the financial pressures faced by families.

## Context Surrounding the Fee Increases

The adjustments come at a time when many families are already budgeting for various education-related expenses, including tuition, uniforms, and extracurricular activities. The increase in transport fees adds another layer of financial consideration for parents planning for the school year ahead.

In discussions about the challenges in the educational sector, it has been highlighted that rising fuel costs have been particularly burdensome for transport providers. Despite these pressures, some schools are reportedly working to keep fee increases as minimal as possible. This reflects a broader awareness within the educational community of the financial strains that families are experiencing.

## Implications for Families

For parents, the increase in transport fees means revisiting their back-to-school budgets and potentially adjusting their financial plans. As transport costs become a more significant part of educational expenses, families may need to consider their options carefully, including evaluating different transport providers or alternative arrangements.
